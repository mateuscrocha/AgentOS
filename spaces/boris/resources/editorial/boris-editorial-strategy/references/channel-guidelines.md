# Channel Guidelines

## YouTube

Use for deeper technical material, especially when the goal is to teach operational reasoning, show workflows, or explain how Bóris reads signal inside message volume.

Prioritize:

- technical breakdowns
- workflow walkthroughs
- scenario-led explanations
- comparisons between manual operation and assisted operation

Structure:

- open with the operational problem
- name the decision or visibility gap
- break the explanation into steps, criteria, or framework
- close with practical application and CTA

Editorial guidance:

- teach through a concrete operational scenario, not abstract theory
- prefer "how to think" and "how to diagnose" over feature listing
- keep the tone practical, analytical, and calm
- avoid exaggerated hooks or generic creator-style energy
- when the subject is technical, depth matters more than cadence

Technical material rules:

- start from a real workflow, bottleneck, or diagnostic question
- define terms before using them repeatedly
- separate symptom, cause, and operational consequence
- show what changes in decision-making, not only what the product does
- use examples, mock situations, or before/after reasoning whenever possible

## Instagram

This is the default primary channel for Bóris editorial output unless the user requests another priority.

Prioritize:

- carousels
- checklists
- before and after narratives
- short educational series

Structure:

- first slide with tension or pain
- middle slides with explanation
- last slide with takeaway and CTA

Editorial guidance:

- lead with a recognizable pain or operational tension
- keep each slide focused on one idea
- prefer concise lines over dense paragraphs
- end with a save, share, or DM CTA

### Instagram Stories

Use Stories as the daily proximity layer for Bóris, especially for short observations, routine signals, audience interaction, and lighter continuity around bigger feed or Reels pieces.

Official format name:

- Cabeça Fria

Editorial role:

- Boris in everyday life
- short operational readings
- lighter contrast to Cabeça Quente
- daily presence and continuity

Prioritize:

- short observational lines
- fast diagnostic cues
- polls, question boxes, and lightweight interaction
- story-native tone over polished ad language

Visual rules:

- keep the full Boris image visible in the background whenever possible
- overlay text directly on the image instead of separating the layout into independent top and bottom blocks
- use white text with a black or very dark semi-transparent background shape behind each line or behind the full text block when contrast requires it
- prefer clean sans-serif type with high legibility and correct Portuguese accents
- keep text short, with generous internal padding and visible breathing room between text and background shape
- when using line-by-line text treatment, leave clear vertical spacing between lines so the story feels light and readable
- preserve safe margins near the top and sides so Instagram interface elements do not compete with the copy
- avoid decorative typography, serif fonts, elaborate editorial layouts, and unnecessary labels
- the result should feel like a native Instagram Story, not like a polished ad banner

Recommended text treatment for approved Boris Stories:

- background image: full-frame Boris visual
- text color: white
- text backing: black rounded rectangle with medium-to-high opacity
- composition: top-left placement by default
- copy style: one short thought per line, 2 to 4 lines max
- visual goal: immediate reading, strong contrast, and minimal friction

## LinkedIn

Use as the default secondary adaptation channel.

Prioritize:

- reframed versions of Instagram posts
- operator-focused observations
- category positioning
- product and workflow examples

Adaptation guidance:

- convert slide logic into a compact argument
- keep more nuance and context than Instagram
- use a stronger thesis and practical conclusion
- end with a softer CTA than Instagram when appropriate

Structure:

- strong opening statement
- practical breakdown
- clear conclusion
- CTA for comment, DM, or demo

## WhatsApp

Use only occasionally, mainly for direct distribution, quick proof, or invitation.

Prioritize:

- short text
- quick insight
- direct invitation
- concise proof or example

Structure:

- direct opener
- one useful point
- one concrete next step
