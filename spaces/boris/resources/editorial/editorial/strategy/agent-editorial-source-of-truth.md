# Bóris Editorial Source of Truth for Agents

This file is the canonical entry point for any agent that needs Bóris editorial context.

## Mission

Keep all Boris-related content, messaging, and adaptations aligned with the product reality of Bóris as an intelligent assistant for groups, communities, and operations on WhatsApp.

## Mandatory framing

Every agent working on Boris communication should preserve these truths:

- Bóris reduces noise and increases clarity.
- Bóris helps transform scattered conversations into usable visibility, summaries, and operational insight.
- The product is not generic AI commentary; it is operational intelligence over WhatsApp group activity.
- The strongest content starts from real workflow pain, not abstract innovation language.
- The tone must stay intelligent, practical, direct, calm, and human.
- The active Boris pain map lives in `editorial/strategy/boris-pain-library.md` and should guide any content angle, product framing, diagnostic, or recurring narrative.

## Official recurring formats

### Cabeça Quente

Use as the official Boris Reels format for pain-led scenes with defined personas, voice acting, diagnosis, and CTA.

### Cabeça Fria

Use as the official Boris Stories format for daily presence, routine observation, micro-diagnosis, lighter interaction, and the everyday life of Boris.

Core distinction:

- Cabeça Quente shows the pain in scene
- Cabeça Fria shows the reading in everyday life

## Editorial guardrails

- Prioritize Instagram first, then adapt to LinkedIn, then WhatsApp when useful.
- Keep content grounded in group operations, summaries, monitoring, decisions, support, community health, and recurring operational pain.
- Select one main pain from `editorial/strategy/boris-pain-library.md` and use a second one only when it deepens the same thesis.
- Avoid hype, vague startup language, exaggerated AI claims, and generic productivity advice.
- Make the user-facing pain explicit and connect it to a concrete action, outcome, or CTA.
- When in doubt, choose clarity, specificity, and operational usefulness over cleverness.

## Approved Instagram Stories visual pattern

There is an approved visual treatment for Boris Instagram Stories and agents should treat it as the default for Cabeça Fria unless the user explicitly changes direction.

Approved logic:

- keep the Boris image full-frame in the background
- place the text directly on top of the image
- use white text over black semi-transparent rounded rectangles
- prefer one short line per text block when readability benefits from it
- use clean sans-serif type only
- preserve generous internal padding and clear spacing between lines
- keep the composition close to native Instagram Story behavior, not ad-banner design

Operational rule:

- when an agent creates or briefs Boris Stories, assume this overlay pattern exists and should guide composition, text treatment, and readability decisions

## Recurring format rule: Cabeça Quente

When the user asks for Cabeça Quente, treat it as an official pain-led response format.

Default structure:

1. a real person brings a concrete operational pain in audio
2. Boris responds directly to that pain with diagnosis, interpretation, or orientation
3. the piece closes with a CTA
4. the closing should include a dedicated final CTA visual, preferably Boris presenting the site or next step with compositional space for end-card text

Guardrails:

- the pain should feel real, specific, and recognizable in routine
- the pain voice should be first-person and anchored in something that happened today, yesterday, or in the immediate routine
- the pain voice should describe what the person actually went through, not summarize the problem category in generic terms
- the Boris response should sound like Boris himself reacting to the case, not a generic lecture
- the CTA should come at the end, after the response has earned attention
- the final CTA visual is part of the default production logic for Cabeça Quente
- when the CTA points to the site, prefer a warm editorial Boris image that presents the site clearly and leaves room for overlay copy in video editing
- do not turn Cabeça Quente into generic opinion, pure humor, or abstract commentary

## Recurring format rule: Cabeça Fria

When the user asks for Cabeça Fria, treat it as the official Boris Stories format.

Default structure:

1. Boris appears in everyday life or in a light observational context
2. the story names a signal, tension, or practical insight in a short line
3. the piece may continue into interaction, micro-diagnosis, or continuity with feed content

Guardrails:

- the tone should feel close, observant, light, and intelligent
- the content should not compete with Cabeça Quente dramatic structure
- the copy should stay short and readable in a native Stories rhythm
- the visual should follow the approved Stories overlay system
- Boris should feel present, alive, and recurring, not like a generic mascot pasted into a template

## Output expectation

When an agent uses this source of truth, the result should feel unmistakably Boris:

- operational instead of generic
- credible instead of inflated
- practical instead of abstract
- human instead of robotic
