# Boris Commercial Context

## Sprint Goal
Support a focused 30-day commercial push to sell Boris with higher clarity, stronger process, and better conversion.

## What Is Already True
- Boris has strong perceived value when people see it working in real groups.
- The product has been validated organically across communities and known operators.
- Sales so far have depended too much on:
  - networking
  - indications
  - people seeing Boris in action
- The sales process is still concentrated in the founder.

## Main Commercial Bottleneck
The bottleneck is not "does Boris solve something real?".
The bottleneck is "how do we turn interest and proof into a repeatable sales motion?".

## Most Important Strategic Insights
- The best sales trigger is contextual demonstration, not abstract explanation.
- Selective trial/group entry is stronger than public free trial.
- Ticket has likely been underpriced relative to perceived value.
- The business needs two tracks:
  - fast revenue opportunities
  - strategic lighthouse accounts
- Follow-up discipline matters because many high-value buyers are busy, not necessarily disinterested.

## Priority Commercial Tracks

### 1. Fast Revenue
Focus on buyers who understand the problem quickly and can buy with less friction.

Likely fits:
- communities
- mentors
- infoproduct operators
- education businesses
- creators with active student/customer groups

### 2. Strategic Accounts
Focus on accounts that can unlock many groups or whole categories.

Examples discussed:
- franchises
- large community operators
- partner ecosystems
- enterprise-like operators with multiple groups

### 3. Proof and Narrative
Strengthen:
- cases
- screenshots
- testimonials
- before/after stories
- pricing anchor
- "for whom Boris is not"

## Core Offer Narrative
Boris is most compelling when framed as:
- time saved
- clarity gained
- community intelligence
- less loss inside message volume
- better decisions from group activity
- more engagement, retention, and business insight

## Sales Style Fit
- WhatsApp-first
- short qualification
- lightweight consultative conversation
- contextual demo
- clear next step
- minimal friction

## Risks to Watch
- Trying to serve too many ICPs at once
- Mixing strategic long-cycle accounts with urgent cash needs
- Using rigid, over-scripted sales language
- Leaving follow-up loose because the buyer is senior or hard to reach
- Offering free access too broadly
